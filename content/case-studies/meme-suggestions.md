## Overview
Identified a behavioral gap in the post-editing flow — users wanted to attach memes but had to search externally — and built an in-product meme recommendation system that drove 2M+ daily page views within weeks of launch.

## Background
As part of a broader initiative to improve posting frequency on QQ Space, we noticed a growing trend: meme culture was becoming a dominant form of self-expression among users. Posts with images consistently outperformed text-only posts in engagement. But the process of adding a meme was cumbersome: users had to leave the app, search online, download the image, then upload it manually.

## Insight
The behavior was already there — users wanted to post memes. The friction was purely operational. If we could bring the meme library directly into the post-editing flow, we could improve both the quality of posts and the overall posting experience without changing user intent at all.

## What we built
- Collected and tagged a library of the most popular memes with relevant keywords
- During post editing, the system analyzed text input in real time and surfaced relevant meme suggestions automatically
- Users could select and insert a meme with one tap — no app switching, no downloading
- Two interaction modes: browse by category, or search by keyword

## Results
- Feature reached 2M+ daily page views and drove 1.5M+ daily posts within weeks of launch
- Trending topic keywords (TV dramas, seasonal events) drove the highest engagement
- Laid the foundation for ongoing keyword and content optimization based on trending topics

## What I learned
The best UX improvements don't change what users want to do — they eliminate the steps standing between the user and the action they already intend to take. In this case, we didn't invent meme culture. We just got out of its way.
