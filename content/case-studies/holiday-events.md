## Overview
Delivered a high-impact Christmas engagement feature for 7M users in 3 days by making a deliberate prioritization call, introducing an external vendor model to stretch limited engineering resources across two simultaneous holiday initiatives.

## Background
Every major holiday is an engagement opportunity for social platforms — but also a resource constraint problem. In late 2015, Christmas and New Year's fell within days of each other, and we had one engineering team to support both.

## The Trade-off
The instinct was to do both fully. But splitting resources evenly would have meant two mediocre experiences instead of one strong one. I pushed for a deliberate prioritization call based on two factors:
- **User preference**: our platform's core demographic skewed toward younger users who were more culturally engaged with Christmas than New Year's
- **Competitive context**: it was peak holiday season, and competing platforms were already building festive atmospheres. Going into Christmas with nothing would create a visible gap — users would notice the absence

The decision: invest fully in Christmas, and handle New Year's with a lighter-touch atmosphere approach rather than a full feature build.

## Solving the resource problem
Prioritizing Christmas didn't solve the engineering capacity problem — the team still needed to support both initiatives simultaneously. To unblock this, I introduced an external vendor model for the first time. The vendor handled the hands-on execution of the Christmas feature — the video template production and interaction details — while our internal team focused on API integration and quality control. This let us run two workstreams in parallel without overloading the internal team.

## What we built
- 3 Santa Claus duo-dance video templates
- Users could upload photos of themselves and friends to be composited into the video
- Shareable with @ tagging, enabling friends to participate and create their own versions
- Launched in 3 days using HTML5

## Results
- 7M users participated
- 45.6% sharing rate — nearly half of all participants actively spread the feature
- Successfully established an external vendor collaboration model reused for future seasonal initiatives

## What I learned
In resource-constrained environments, the best PM decision is often not how to do everything — it's how to make the most important thing excellent. Execution capacity is a variable you can expand, not just a constraint you have to accept.
